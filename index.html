This poem is all about a girl whom the author is not knowing about. The author saw her singing on the way he crosses by and is addicted to her voice because her voice completely removes his tierdness and refreshes his mind. He tries to explain his fellow mates about her singing by hearing her words.

Nobody knows exactly about her singing. However, according to author, her voice seems to send a message about some of her familiar pain that she has suffered with and wanna remove her sorrow by singing a song.

THE SOLITARY MISSY


Behold her, sitting alone,
A shy, beautiful and adorable lass.
Lost in her own thinkings,
So wait for her, or gently pass.

Alone she thinks about her pain,
Which let her live in strain.
O listen! her song's sound,
That is overflowing from the ground.

A voice so thrilling, never I've heard,
At anytime from any bird.
Which makes me feel proud,
By breaking the noise of the crowd.

Will no one tell me what she sings,
Or should I go and listen to her.
Perheps some of her familiar loss or pain,
That has happened and may be again.

Whatever the theme she may sings,
May her voice deliver what she is sending.
I saw a beauty, to be given a friendship ring,
So that none of her pain may be pending.

However, I have to go on my path,
Leaving the sound which refreshed my heart.
Hope her singing may remove her sore,
But long after, I heard her voice no more.


--VIVEK KUMAR
